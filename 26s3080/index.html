GD1

Description

This course is an introduction to fundamental concepts of graphic design theory and practice including: visual language, narrative, analysis, and working within and for various contexts.

The goals are to create visuals for different contexts and purposes; apply different tools, styles, and approaches to communicate meaning with form (P1); present research and rationale for design decisions (P2); recognize and articulate the ways design impact society and the world (P3); Evaluate work for appropriateness, effectiveness, and engagement; identify and apply aspects of self-identity to the process and work.

Projects

∴ Animal Poster

This project develops an understanding of visual literacy through a process of iteration and exploration of imagemaking techniques and artistic styles. Create a series of visual messages that demonstrate levels of visual data from representational and abstract on a grid-based, 17 x 22 inch poster, vector graphics.

▬ Subculture Zine

Choose a subculture or community and craft a visual narrative in the form of a zine that demonstrates complexity using sequencing and layering of words and images.

▰ Collaborative Kiosk or Alterego

Understand context using scale as a principle. How design is used in interaction with each other and the world. Identify a social or political issue. Design a kiosk with an interactive element. Results in a presentation with infographics.

◴ Self Presentation

Apply aspects of self-identity in a presentation of a meta-analysis of the processes and works from the semester.
